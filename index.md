## Abstract

Unmanned vending machines (UVMs) with automated recognition systems are emerging as seamless shopping solutions. However, existing recognition systems often rely on cumbersome tagging and shelf-wise camera installations, which increase operational burden and limit layout flexibility, respectively. To alleviate these limitations, this paper proposes a two-camera purchase recognition system that uses only top and bottom views of a UVM. The proposed system recognizes purchase actions by combining hand-centered detection, complementary integration of the two views, and robust hand tracking tailored to the constrained vending space. Experiments on a prototype UVM show that the proposed system achieves an average F1-score of 0.922 over 929 actions while operating at 33 frames per second on low-cost hardware. These results demonstrate the feasibility of efficient purchase recognition for practical UVM deployment.
